## Scope

This section documents client-facing compliance surfaces in the native app, including in-app legal policy presentation, pack-opening disclosure requirements, and app-review alignment.

## Included topics

- In-app legal document presentation (Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, EULA)
- Policy copy synchronization between native app and marketing site
- Pack odds integration from backend payloads
- Disclosure copy and accessibility constraints
- App-review evidence requirements for release submissions

## In-app legal policy documents

The native app presents three legal documents in-app through the **Settings > Data & Privacy > Legal** section:

| Document | Constant file | Effective date |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Terms of Service** | `constants/terms-of-service.ts` | April 3, 2026 |
| **Privacy Policy** | `constants/privacy-policy.ts` | April 3, 2026 |
| **EULA** | `constants/eula.ts` | April 3, 2026 |

### Presentation

Legal documents are rendered using the `TextContentModal` component (`components/settings/modals/TextContentModal.tsx`). This modal parses plain-text policy content into a structured card layout with:

- An effective-date info card
- Numbered section cards with bullet support
- A "last updated" footer

The `DataSettings` tab (`components/settings/tabs/DataSettings.tsx`) wires each document into a `TextContentModal` instance and also provides "Open in Browser" links to the corresponding pages on the marketing site (`d-sports.org`).

### Sync requirements

In-app legal copy must stay in sync with the website versions hosted on `d-sports-site`:

- **Website pages:** `/terms-of-service`, `/privacy-policy`, `/eula`
- **Native constants:** `constants/terms-of-service.ts`, `constants/privacy-policy.ts`, `constants/eula.ts`

When policy text is updated, you must update both the native constants and the website page content to keep them aligned. Each constant file includes a comment reminding contributors of this requirement.

### EULA acceptance tracking

The app tracks EULA acceptance via `user.ugcTermsAcceptedAt` and `user.ugcTermsVersion`. The `DataSettings` component compares the stored version against `EULA_VERSION` to display acceptance status.
