## Feature parity with backend/PWA

`d-sports-engage-native` targets high parity with `d-sports-api` feature behavior while optimizing for mobile UX patterns.

## Wallet and pack opening

- Wallet screens consume balances, token data, and secure key/pin flows from backend APIs.
- Pack browsing, purchase, and open flows use backend-driven states and inventory refresh behavior.
- Odds-disclosure surfaces read backend-provided probability payloads.

## Quest, rewards, and pass surfaces

- Quest UI supports team-aware progression and pass-gated eligibility.
- Reward states and claim flows follow backend quest/reward APIs.
- Global pass and event pass effects are reflected in native quest visibility/completion UX.

## Team and leaderboard integration

- Teams and leagues metadata are consumed from backend `/api/teams` and `/api/leagues`.
- **Per-team leaderboard routes** — `/leaderboard/[teamSlug]` and `/leaderboard/global` load boards by `teamId` (not a stale numeric `leaderboardId`), matching quest and sync data.
- **MMKV cache separation** — Team detail "Top fans" and the full leaderboard use distinct cache shapes so opening a modal before the full screen no longer shows an empty board.
- **Official team label** — Teams with `isOfficial: true` and no league show "Official" instead of "Other".
- Favorite-team interactions influence quest and leaderboard context where backend eligibility requires it.

## Locker room games

- **Dedicated game routes** — Prize Wheel (`/locker-room/wheel-spin`) and Guess the Player (`/locker-room/player-guess`) open as stack routes, eliminating z-order issues from nested modals.
- Spin completion and equipment claim flows are hardened with segment/outcome alignment and proper `isSpinning` reset.

## Points history

- Profile surfaces consume `GET /api/user/points-history` so game awards (wheel, guess-the-player) appear alongside quest completions.
- The response includes a derived `kind` field (quest, game, onboarding, other) for categorization.
